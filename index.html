Sunnyvale Math Club

Overview

What are math competitions?

  • A series of math problems are given, often to solve within a certain time limit
  • Competitions can be multiple choice, short answer, or consist of proof questions where a full, rigorous explanation must be provided
  • There are both team AND individual contests
  • Problems mainly fall under four categories: Algebra, Geometry, Combinatorics (counting, probability, etc), and Number Theory (primes, modular arithmetic, etc)

Why math competitions?

  • Often the math is not covered in school, which makes it a great way to learn about different fields
  • Builds problem solving skills applicable to the real world
  • Hang out with friends and have fun!

History

Math club initially acted as a way for Sunnyvale Middle School to send a team to Mathcounts. It was teacher led: Mr. Robinson printed handouts, and opened his room during lunch for groups of students to work together, and took students to the Mathcounts competition. As with many clubs, Math Club died during Covid.

This year, we are reviving the club and making it entirely high school volunteer led. We will also focus more on preparation for various competitions, and send teams to Mathcounts and Berkeley Mini Math Tournament (BMMT).
